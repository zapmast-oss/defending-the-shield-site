David Thompson Bashes Three over the Wall
Friday, April 12th , 2002
David Thompson was sending souvenirs to the sun gods all game long in the 6-1 New Orleans Saints triumph at Louisiana Superdome.

The 36-year-old put on a show for fans, launching not one, not two, but three home runs into the stands and powering the Saints past the Atlanta Falcons.

"One homer's good, two's great, three's out of sight," Thompson told BNN reporters after the game. "I can't wait until next game. I really feel good at the plate."

The shortstop went 3-for-4, rung up 6 RBIs and scored 3 runs on his record-setting day.

David Thompson hit a two-run home run off Matt Maxwell in the 2nd, hit a two-run home run off Matt Maxwell in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Matt Maxwell in the 6th and flied out in the 8th.

This season, Thompson is hitting at a .325 pace with 6 home runs, 12 RBIs and has scored 8 times.
Salamone Baffles Kansas City Batters
Friday, April 12th , 2002
Magic was in the air and on the mound at RCA Dome, as Danny Salamone held the Kansas City Chiefs spellbound in his 1-0 shutout victory. The Indianapolis Colts pitcher went the distance, rang up 12 strikeouts, walked 0, and held the Chiefs to 4 hits.

"He just had us in his thrall," Kansas City manager Pat Harshberger said. "We couldn't put anything together against him."
