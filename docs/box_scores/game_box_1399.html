Giants Fall, 13-7, to Trewartha, Commanders
Washington catcher Jake Trewartha contributed significantly to his team's 13-7 win over the New York Giants. Trewartha went 4 for 5 with 3 runs scored to lead the offensive attack for the Commanders. The Commanders, now 10-3, have been playing some good baseball.

The Giants had been locked in a 6-6 tie with the Commanders going into the bottom of the sixth inning. That changed quickly when the 24-year old shortstop Jason Earls came to the plate. Earls hit a bases-clearing triple on a 2-2 fastball from New York hurler Juan Garza. It was Earls's 1st triple of the year.

"It doesn't matter if you're 10 games over .500 or 10 games under, it feels good to win," Trewartha told the Washington Today.
