Copeland Stars as Bears Top Saints, 13-9
In a high-scoring game between Chicago and New Orleans, center fielder Tristan Copeland was a difference-maker. The 32-year-old helped Chicago grab the 13-9 win. He was 3 for 3 with a home run, a double, and 2 walks. He scored 4 runs and drove in 2. Harry McDonald was the winner. He's now 3-1. The loser was 30-year old right-handed pitcher Sam Davis. The win puts Chicago at 11-7.

Andy Miller had a big 2-run home run for the Bears in the bottom of the first inning. With Chicago trailing 1-0 and a runner on 1st, he sized up a changeup from 30-year old right-handed pitcher Sam Davis and punished it. The result was his 2nd home run of the season, which put the Bears on top, 2-1.

"We're playing like we're capable of," said Chicago manager Gregg Bianchino.
