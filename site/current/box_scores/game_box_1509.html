LeMond Helps Green Bay Bombard San Francisco
The Green Bay Packers blasted the San Francisco 49ers, 14-4. Elliott LeMond was one of the hitting stars. He was 3-4 with a home run, 2 singles, and a walk, while scoring 3 runs and driving in 2 for Green Bay. Brandon Henkes notched the win in relief. The right-hander allowed no runs on no hits in 1.2 innings. His record now sits at 1-1. The win puts Green Bay at 15-12.

The 49ers had been locked in a 4-4 tie with the Packers going into the bottom of the fifth inning. That changed quickly when the 27-year old catcher Craig Mazza came to the plate. Mazza hit a 2-run double on a 0-1 cutter from San Francisco hurler Oscar Cabrales. It was Mazza's 6th double of the year.

"We're trying to go out there and win games," LeMond told the Green Bay Daily News, "and we played pretty well tonight."
